I am a fourth-year PhD student in the Information Sciences at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), advised by Nigel Bosch and Jana Diesner.

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning/AI, human-computer interaction, and cognitive science. I explore how people learn, think, and interact in computer-based learning environments. Specifically, I am interested in (1) developing eXplainable AI (XAI)-driven interventions that support various facets of metacognition (e.g., self-regulated learning or metacognitive calibration) and (2) uncovering biases within predictive models to ensure AI fairness for diverse groups of people (e.g., individuals diagnosed with ADHD). I leverage XAI, causal inference, controlled experiments, and surveys, and I am also interested in using LLMs.

I received my bachelor's in Mathematics and Statistics from UIUC, advised by A.J. Hildebrand.
